Frequently Asked Questions

What does the SNOOZ Pro sound like? +
SNOOZ Pro sounds like a fan — because it has a real fan inside. You can adjust the tone from a light, airy fan sound (similar to a table fan) to a deep, low-pitched hum (similar to an airplane cabin) using the tone control knob on the bottom. The sound is continuous, natural, and never loops or repeats.
How loud is the SNOOZ Pro? +
SNOOZ Pro has 10 volume levels and produces sound ranging from 46 to 87 dBA measured at the device. It's powerful enough to mask noisy neighbors, city street noise, snoring, barking dogs, tinnitus, and office conversations. For best results, place it 3–6 feet from your head, between you and the noise source.
What's the difference between the Pro and the Original? +
Both use a real fan inside to generate natural, non-looping white noise. The Pro adds an upgraded build and fabric design, a new easy-set tone control knob on the bottom, expanded app functionality, and a protective travel case included. The sound quality and volume range (46–87 dBA) are the same across both models.
Do I need the app to use it? +
No. SNOOZ Pro works perfectly on its own — just plug it in and adjust the volume and tone manually. The free SNOOZ app (iOS and Android) is completely optional and adds remote control, scheduling with fade in/out, night light mode, nursery calibration, and button brightness control. There are no subscriptions — ever.
Is the SNOOZ Pro safe for babies? +
Many parents use the SNOOZ Pro in their nursery. The free app includes a nursery calibration feature that limits the volume to a level aligned with pediatric guidelines. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends placing sound machines at least 7 feet from your baby's sleeping area and keeping the volume comfortable.

Yes. The SNOOZ Pro is HSA and FSA eligible — you can use your pre-tax health savings or flexible spending account benefits to purchase it.
